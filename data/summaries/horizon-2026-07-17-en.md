## [Firefox Browser Compiled to WebAssembly Runs Inside Chrome](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jul/16/firefox-in-webassembly/#atom-everything) ⭐️ 8.0/10

The project Puter has successfully compiled the entire Firefox/Gecko browser engine to WebAssembly, allowing it to run as a fully functional browser within another browser like Chrome. This achievement is a significant demonstration of WebAssembly's potential to run complex, entire applications, pushing the boundaries of what is possible on the web and showcasing a creative use of AI tools for large-scale software compilation tasks. The team chose Firefox/Gecko for its strong single-process support and used the Wisp protocol to tunnel all network traffic through a WebSocket proxy due to browser security restrictions. The project reportedly used about $25,000 worth of Claude Opus and Fable tokens, though actual costs were lower via a subscription plan.

The Wisp protocol is a system for multiplexing TCP/UDP streams over a single WebSocket connection, often used to tunnel traffic. Claude is a series of large language models from Anthropic, with Opus and Fable being specific models used for different tasks in AI-assisted development.

## [OpenRAG Corpus for EU AI Act Released](https://www.reddit.com/r/MachineLearning/comments/1uytlac/eu_ai_act_openrag_933_legally_structured_chunks/) ⭐️ 8.0/10

A new structured corpus of the EU AI Act (Regulation (EU) 2024/1689) has been released, optimized for Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG). The corpus chunks the text by legal structure rather than sliding windows, creating 933 chunks with BGE-M3 embeddings stored in a single SQLite database. This resource provides a high-quality, domain-specific dataset for AI governance and legal NLP, addressing the growing need to make complex regulations machine-readable and actionable for AI systems. By benchmarking against a baseline, it demonstrates how structural chunking can improve retrieval tasks like article recall, which is crucial for reducing hallucinations in legal AI applications. The SQLite database stores 1024-dimensional BGE-M3 embeddings for each chunk and includes metadata like EUR-Lex links, application dates, and derived labels (with NULLs for ambiguous cases). Benchmark results showed the structural corpus achieved a scenario article recall@20 of 0.541 versus the baseline's 0.449, though overall RAG classification performance was similar.

reddit · r/MachineLearning · /u/Automatic-Forever-63 · Jul 17, 08:18

**Background**: Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) is a technique that enhances large language models by retrieving relevant information from external knowledge sources before generating a response. BGE-M3 is a multilingual embedding model that converts text into numerical vectors for similarity search. Legal NLP often uses RAG to ensure AI models ground their answers in authoritative legal texts like the EU AI Act, reducing factual errors.

## [First Atmosphere Detected on Earth-like Planet in Habitable Zone](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cy4kdd1e0ejo) ⭐️ 7.0/10

Scientists using the James Webb Space Telescope have detected the first confirmed atmosphere on a rocky, Earth-sized exoplanet, LHS 1140b, which is located in the habitable zone of a red dwarf star 48 light-years away. The atmosphere is primarily composed of helium. This discovery provides the first strong evidence that a rocky planet in the habitable zone of a red dwarf can retain an atmosphere, which is a crucial prerequisite for potential surface conditions that might support life. It validates the capability of current telescopes to characterize the atmospheres of distant, potentially habitable worlds. The planet LHS 1140b has about 5.6 times Earth's mass and 70% larger radius, classifying it as a super-Earth, and it orbits entirely within its star's habitable zone receiving 43% of Earth's incident flux. While the helium atmosphere is notable, the planet's close orbit to its active red dwarf star raises questions about atmospheric loss and long-term stability.

hackernews · neversaydie · Jul 17, 14:06 · [Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48947560)

**Background**: Red dwarf stars are cooler and dimmer than our Sun, so their habitable zones are much closer to the star, where planets are often tidally locked and subject to intense stellar flares and winds that can strip away atmospheres. Detecting an atmosphere on a rocky exoplanet, especially in such a challenging environment, is a major technical achievement made possible by telescopes like JWST that can analyze starlight filtering through a planet's atmosphere during transits.
